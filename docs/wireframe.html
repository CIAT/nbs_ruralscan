Land slope

Topographic · cluster representative (3 variables in cluster) · GEE native
What it is
Rate of elevation change across the landscape, expressed in degrees from horizontal. Derived from a 30 m Digital Elevation Model.
Why it's included (agroforestry-specific)
Slope controls runoff, root anchoring, mechanisation feasibility, and erosion risk for tree-crop establishment. Steep slopes constrain implementation; very flat areas may waterlog.
How to read it
0–8° (gentle): highly suitable · 8–20°: moderately suitable, terracing may help · >25°: generally unsuitable for tree-crop agroforestry without engineering. Trapezoidal fuzzy membership applied.
What it represents (correlation cluster)
In this AOI (Sierra Leone), slope correlates strongly (|r| > 0.7) with these variables, which it represents in the MCDA:
Aspect (r = 0.78) Curvature (r = 0.81) TWI · topo wetness (r = 0.73)

You can promote any of these to its own row, or pick a different cluster representative, in technical mode.

Where it comes from
NATIVE GEESRTM v3, 30 m · global · USGS/SRTMGL1_003 · CC0

Pre-processed at 1 km via mean resampling for the analysis layer. Resampled view marked in the resolution audit.
